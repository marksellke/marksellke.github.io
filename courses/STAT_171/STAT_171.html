STAT 171: Stochastic Processes
Spring 2025

Stochastic processes are probabilistic models that describe how systems evolve randomly over time. They are fundamental tools in modern statistics, providing frameworks for understanding stock market fluctuations, gene expression, climate modeling and more. This course develops the theoretical foundations of stochastic processes. We will explore how randomness and time interact in complex systems, building up from finite Markov chains to sophisticated continuous-time processes like Brownian motion.
